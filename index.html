Research

Selected Threads

Dropout universality and scheduling

Derived a mean-field theory of dropout in stochastic neural-network dynamics, predicting activation-dependent scaling laws and depth-dependent schedules. With the objective of conserving signal propagation, these schedules outperform standard constant dropout in both accuracy and loss, achieving up to 35.4% lower test loss and 65% lower compute cost at matched accuracy across MLP, ResNet, and ViT models on CIFAR-10/100. Read the paper.

Robust AI debate and aggregation

Built experiments for adversarial LLM debate under asymmetric information, comparing majority vote, linear pooling, logarithmic pooling, Hedge-style updates, and cost-accuracy Pareto frontiers. Pooled weaker-model ensembles achieved higher accuracy at approximately 70% lower inference cost.

Tensor networks and scientific computing

Built Python workflows for large-scale tensor-network simulations with Prof. Tarnopolsky, running DMRG experiments with scaling analysis and convergence diagnostics for lattice-model observables.

Effective field theory and gravity

Developed symmetry-based EFT constructions using coset methods, tensor calculus, and algebraic geometry, with applications to entropy bounds for irrelevant operators, broken Weyl gravity, generalized Proca theory, and vector torsion.
